About Me

A Self-Taught Front-End Web Developer with a Passion for Problem Solving.
After completing my associate's degree in pre-nursing, I realized that medicine wasn't for me and decided to give coding a shot. I chose coding because I have always had an interest in technology, yet I knew nothing about code, and that intrigued me. I was successful in teaching myself how to code using online resources and creating personal projects. As soon as I made a rock-paper-scissors game using JavaScript, I knew that I developed a passion for web development. I would say that I am a highly disciplined and persistent person. Those two traits have been integral to my success as a self-taught developer, as I had to wrap my head around very difficult concepts every day. I enjoy learning and I am always browsing the web looking for new technologies that could make me a stronger developer.

My problem solving skills are something that I am really proud of, and have allowed me to make many exciting projects. Coding things like a calculator, a todo list, or a tic-tac-toe game seemed like they would be simple, but they really put my ability to solve problems to the test! Fortunately, I have been able to develop those skills not only through learning how to code, but also through 4+ years training martial arts. Throughout those four years, I had always found it extremely helpful to break down a big problem into smaller ones, and go from there. That strategy has also proven itself to be extremely effective while coding. The one thing I really want to drive home about me is that I am a driven and hardworking individual, and I am committed to delivering exceptional results in everything I do. I would love to bring my skills and passion for web development to your team.
